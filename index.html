The gap is real

Most parents only discover
their child's gaps when it's too late.

01
Silent shortcomings
Most parents can't tell if their child's speech, motor skills or focus are on track — until a teacher flags it years later.
02
Finding help is exhausting
Once a gap is spotted, parents spend weeks hunting for the right class or specialist — with no trusted starting point.
03
Great services stay hidden
Talented coaches, therapists and teachers have no easy way to reach the parents who are actively looking for them right now.
04
Online vs offline confusion
Parents don't know which activities need to be in-person and which work over video — so they default to neither and the child misses out.
